Drug abuse among youths is when young people use drugs like alcohol, cigarettes, or illegal substances in ways that harm their health or life.

It’s serious because it can cause addiction, health problems, and poor school performance, often starting due to peer pressure, stress, or family issues.

Causes     Causes     Causes

Drug use among youths can be caused by several factors including peer pressure, family problems, and stress. Some young people may also become curious about drugs or be influenced by media and friends who make drug use seem normal.

A good course of action to reduce drug abuse among youths is to increase education and awareness about the dangers of drugs. Parents, teachers, and counselors should guide and support young people while youth programs can help them cope with stress and peer pressure in healthy ways.

Effects

Drug abuse can harm young people’s brain development, cause addiction, and affect memory and concentration. It can also lead to poor school performance, risky behavior, and mental health problems like anxiety or depression.
